## The Quartet — one generation, one crisis

Morimorty, Hesianor, Hippotes, and Halthir were **challengers of the same Trials generation** — four friends who adventured together. During their Trials year, a Shadow Pokémon crisis struck Laekon; Morimorty ended it at a devastating personal cost, was recognized by Mew, and became the Hero of Laekon and its last Heartwarden. The other three rose to become the Wardens under him.

**The blind spot (story-critical):** all three Wardens trust Morimorty deep in their hearts. Each can feel that *something in him changed* after the crisis — but loyalty never lets them finish the thought. You don't suspect the friend who saved your life at twenty. Their blindness is love, not foolishness; it is his best camouflage, it is why the betrayal devastates, and it is what saves him at the end.

## Morimorty — the Heartwarden — THE VILLAIN
- **Public role:** Hero of Laekon, head of the Ranger Corps, the only living person Mew ever recognized. Warm, distinguished, soft-spoken, ~50. Universally beloved.
- **Partner:** **Marshadow** — genuinely bonded in his heroic years; a Pokémon drawn to the shadows in people's hearts, living literally in his shadow. It stayed as he fell, never judging, only reflecting. His one remaining true bond — poisoned, enabling, and ultimately his way back.
- **The fall:** the crisis of his youth never left him. Fear became obsession — the legendary must be *controlled*. Through the **Madkings** (a small covert cell) and a serum distilled from Marshadow's shadow energy, he restages the worst day of his life in manufactured miniature, building a public case to seize Mew. As Heartwarden he directs the very investigation into his own attacks. He never makes a villainous speech; he still believes he is the hero. The Mad King.
- **The finale:** two-phase battle — his champion team anchored by Marshadow, then Shadow Mew (purification battle). Defeated not by strength but reached by the bonds that never left: Marshadow, and his three old friends.
- **Battle team:** TBD — Marshadow anchor; shadow/grief motif.
- **Name notes:** echoes *memento mori* and "the Mad King." Always "Morimorty" in-game (distinct from canon Gym Leader Morty).

## The Three Wardens

Appointed guardians of the three Trial zones. Each embodies a pillar of the ranger craft — and one answer to the game's central question: is a bond something you *build*, something you're *given*, or something you *choose to see*?

### Hesianor — Warden of Zone 1 — pillar of COEXISTENCE
*The bond is GIVEN — it's everywhere; nature offers it.*
- **Personality:** very outgoing, talks loudly, laughs like thunder — but with an iron moral compass. Harm an ecosystem in front of him and the jokes stop instantly.
- **Domain:** ecosystems, habitats, and the founding idea of Laekon — humans and Pokémon sharing the land. Zone 1 teaches *why rangers exist* before teaching how to fight.
- **Flavor:** an obsessive fisherman — found rod-in-hand at every body of water; claims fishing is "listening to the region." Home turf: the Tidal Reach.
- **Partner:** Ludicolo (joy incarnate; alt: Whiscash if the fishing theme should be literal)
- **Battle style:** weather and terrain — he fights *with* the environment.
- **Warden mission (TBD):** likely tied to the Tidal Reach fishing-community tensions — mediate between stilt-town fishers and a migrating Pokémon population.
- **Inspiration:** outdoorsy friend — survival games, fishing in every game.

### Hippotes — Warden of Zone 2 — pillar of UNDERSTANDING
*The bond is something you CHOOSE TO SEE — it never shows up in his data, and it quietly delights him.*
- **Personality:** eccentric tech-head. Loves symmetry (symmetrical lab, symmetrical wardrobe, visibly twitches at an uneven bookshelf), optimization, and building the perfect system. Great dialogue voice: "your Pokémon's output is 12% below theoretical optimum — this is *agony* to me."
- **Domain:** knowledge and technique — types, moves, EVs/IVs. His **EV/IV appraisal service** unlocks as an in-game feature in Zone 2.
- **Settopih:** his lab AI assistant — name proudly unexplained until one NPC reads it backwards. Vessel TBD: a Rotom possessing the lab systems, or a Porygon-Z.
- **Plot role:** his analytical instruments are what isolate the synthetic signature in the Professor's Shadow sample — the optimization nerd proves the crisis is manufactured.
- **Partner:** **Metagross** — the supercomputer Pokémon, literally symmetrical, four brains. (Hippowdon survives as an affectionate pun in one line of his dialogue.)
- **Battle style:** ruthless optimization — perfect coverage, engineered synergies.
- **Warden mission (TBD):** field-study / knowledge flavored.
- **Inspiration:** tech friend — AI models on the server (Settopih), Stellaris/Factorio, symmetry.

### Halthir — Warden of Zone 3 — pillar of PROTECTION
*The bond is BUILT — drilled, trained, strong enough to stand behind.*
- **Personality:** calm, composed, serious. The incident commander — when something breaks, his doctrine decides who moves where.
- **Domain:** the Corps' **crisis response and Shadow Pokémon protocols** — he wrote them. No facility needed: his place is the command post. Zone 3, where the Shadow crisis peaks, is supervised by the crisis commander himself.
- **Story role:** personally walks the player through their first close Shadow encounter (a Sanctuary Pokémon triggered by the Madkings) — protocol, taught in the field.
- **Partner:** **Alolan Ninetales** — the Aurora Veil Pokémon: shields the whole team while staying dangerous. A tank/heal main's soul in Pokémon form.
- **Battle style:** the wall before the Grand Final — screens, recovery, bulk that outlasts you while hitting shockingly hard. Punishes players who never learned defense.
- **Warden mission (TBD):** rescue / crisis flavored.
- **Inspiration:** friend who works in crisis/incident management (Lyon); plays tank/heal in every game; strategy & management games.

## Mimi — Elite Ranger, former Trial Champion (creator self-insert)
- **Rank:** Elite Ranger; won the Grand Final of a previous season — **a Trial Champion Mew never came for**. He stood in the Heart of Laekon and the legendary did not appear. He carries the region's wound quietly, the calmest man in the Corps.
- **Partner:** **Dragapult**
- **Role:** the Corps' lead field investigator on the Shadow incidents — reporting to the top, which means his leads are quietly curated by Morimorty himself. The loyal investigator chasing trails laid by the man he reports to.
- **Story beats:** the player crosses him before meeting Halthir — he discusses the situation and leaves to follow a lead on orders from above. Late game: a **duo battle alongside the player**, after which he entrusts them with the final perilous mission — telling them that in his eyes, they are already true rangers. The bond with their Pokémon proves it. Coming from the champion the region's god declined, it lands.
- **Flavor:** past-season Elites like Mimi exist across the region — a retired Elite running a dojo, one who never lived up to the title, an old Elite in the Pale Expanse who remembers when Mew still appeared. Free NPC material.

## The Challenger Cast
Named competitors met at the Catch Ceremony and throughout the circuit. Numbers are public challenger numbers. Losing brackets costs nobody their career — the Wardens watch the whole season (see hierarchy: participation, not victory).

### Challenger 12 — Q
- Hyperactive, determined. He adores his Pokémon and his Pokémon adores him. Together they cook succulent dishes; he dreams of healing Pokémon by making them the best meals.
- **Partner:** Charmander · **Team:** Lapras (+ TBD)

### Challenger 4 — Sanka
- Searching for his path at the start; above all wants to do his best with his Pokémon.
- **Dream:** join the Corps' crisis response teams — first responders facing the forces of nature. **His hero and destination: Warden Halthir's division.**
- **Partner:** Gurdurr

### Challenger 5 — Riri
- Sanka's big sister; only entered because he pushed her to come along.
- **Arc:** her Pokémon doesn't listen and does as it pleases. She must learn to *understand* it instead of commanding — Hesianor's "listen to them" philosophy is a natural thread for her. Breakthrough in a perilous **2v2 alongside the hero against the Madkings operatives who defeated her brother**.
- **Ending:** joins the **Sanctuary's rehabilitation work** under the Professor's institute.
- **Partner:** TBD — must be famously headstrong. Candidates: Mankey line, Bagon, Rockruff (listens only once she stops commanding and starts asking).
