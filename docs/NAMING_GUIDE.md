# Laekon Naming Guide
> How towns, places, and titles are named in Laekon
> Companion to DESIGN_DOCUMENT.md | Last updated: July 2026 (v3)

Laekon's names carry a Belgian/Flemish coastal flavor, echoing the region's real-world inspiration (Laeken, Belgium). Names should feel like real places with linguistic history — short, natural to say out loud, and quietly descriptive.

---

## The System

Every town name = **biome flavor prefix + Flemish geographic suffix**.

- The **suffix is honest**: it always describes the town's actual geography.
- The **prefix carries the biome's sound**: each biome has its own palette of syllables and moods.

Snap one to the other and the name generates itself — and teaches the player what kind of place it is without a single line of dialogue.

---

## Suffix Glossary

| Suffix | Meaning | Use for |
|--------|---------|---------|
| -gem | home, settlement | villages, hometowns |
| -beke | brook | riverside towns |
| -donk | sandy rise in marsh | wetland towns |
| -lo | forest clearing | woodland towns |
| -zele | dwelling, hall | older / grander towns |
| -muide | river mouth | ports, estuaries |
| -dam | dam, crossing | bridge / canal towns |
| -berg | mountain | alpine towns |
| -veld | field, plain | open plains towns |
| -hout | wood, timber | deep-forest towns |

## Craft Rules

1. **Never reuse a prefix twice** across the region — every town keeps a unique first syllable.
2. **The suffix never lies** — a -berg town is on a mountain, a -muide town sits at a river mouth. If the geography changes in a map redesign, the name is wrong, not the map.
3. **One or two towns may break the system** for lore reasons — a name that fits no pattern marks a place as older than the naming tradition itself. Best candidates: the oldest Pale Expanse settlement (tied to the earliest Mew mythology) and the Heart of Laekon.
4. **Say it out loud.** If it doesn't roll off the tongue in an English sentence ("I'm heading to ___"), soften it.
5. **Keep it light.** This is flavor, not linguistics homework. If a name sounds great but bends a rule, the name wins.

## Titles — the final ladder

Titles use plain, evocative English. Seven exist in the whole game, each doing one job:

| Title | What it is |
|-------|-----------|
| **Challenger** | Anyone enrolled in the current Ranger Trials |
| **Ranger Second Class** | Defeated the Warden of Zone 1 |
| **Ranger First Class** | Defeated the Warden of Zone 2 |
| **Elite Ranger** | Defeated the Warden of Zone 3 — a full professional ranger |
| **Warden** | One of three appointed zone guardians (Hesianor, Hippotes, Halthir) |
| **Trial Champion** | Winner of the season's Grand Final — one per year |
| **Heartwarden** | A Trial Champion recognized by Mew in the Heart of Laekon. Rarest title in existence; Morimorty is the only living holder — until the player. |

Other names: **Heart of Laekon** — Mew's hidden dwelling place, kept plain and evocative. **The Madkings** — Morimorty's covert cell. **Soul Squad** — optional public nickname (TBD; candidate: the challenger friend group's name for itself).
